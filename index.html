Come raggiungere il castello?

La soluzione più semplice se si parte da Cesena è usare l'auto (~20 minuti) e prendere la via Emilia. Successivamente si prende via Montiano e si prosegue fino a Montenovo.

Helpful Information for Our International Guests

We're thrilled to have you join us! Here are some tips to make your journey to Cesena as smooth as possible.

How to Reach Cesena

The most convenient airport for reaching Cesena is Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ).

From Bologna to Cesena, your main options are:

  • Train: This is the most recommended option for comfort and speed. From Bologna Airport, take the "Marconi Express" (a short monorail) directly to Bologna Centrale train station (about 7 minutes). From there, frequent trains (Regionale, Intercity) run to Cesena. The train journey from Bologna Centrale to Cesena typically takes between 40 and 60 minutes. You can check schedules and purchase tickets on Trenitalia.

  • Direct Bus: There are also direct bus services (e.g., Shuttle Italy Airport) from Bologna Airport to Cesena. The journey takes approximately 1 hour. Check the bus company websites for schedules and availability.

  • Car Rental: If you prefer more flexibility to explore the region, you can rent a car directly at the airport. The drive takes about 1 hour. Most major car rental companies have desks located directly within the Bologna Airport Terminal, typically on the first floor. While possible to rent upon arrival, booking in advance is strongly advised to ensure availability, get a better price, and avoid long queues, especially for specific car types. Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ) Car Rental

Cesena is also well-connected to smaller airports like Forlì (FRL) and Rimini (RMI), but Bologna is generally the best choice for international connections.

Where to Stay in Cesena

Cesena offers various accommodation options for every budget. We recommend booking in advance, especially if your stay coincides with local events.

Hotels:

For a comfortable stay, look for B&Bs or hotels in the city center or near the train station for easy transport. Some options include:

  • Hotel Casali
  • Hotel Romagna
  • Alternatively, search on platforms like Booking.com or Expedia.
B&Bs (Bed & Breakfasts):

We recommend choosing a B&B over a hotel for a more authentic and personal stay in Cesena. B&Bs often offer a warmer, more welcoming atmosphere, frequently hosted by locals who can provide insider tips and a deeper connection to the area. Many are located in the charming historic center or in peaceful yet well-connected neighborhoods:

  • Mondo Antico (City center)
  • B&B La Garampa (City center, excellent reviews)
  • Armonie B&B (City center)
  • B&B Del Vicolo (Near the train station)
  • Casimiro Rooms (Near the city center)
  • You can find other options on Bed-and-Breakfast.it.
Hostels:

For those looking for a more budget-friendly option, Cesena also has a few hostels, though they are less numerous than in larger cities:

  • Some options might be slightly outside Cesena (e.g., in Rimini or Cesenatico), but check their proximity to public transport.
  • Search on sites like Hostelworld or Travelocity Hostels.
